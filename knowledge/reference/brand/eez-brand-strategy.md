# EEZ Brand Strategy

**Status:** Draft — internal working document
**Owner:** Armagan Ercan (Ecosystem Relations)
**Last updated:** 2026-05-29

## The ownable claim

**Ethereum governing itself economically, not just technically.**

Every competitor is a foundation-led infrastructure project that asks Ethereum to adopt it. EEZ is the only initiative whose authority derives from Ethereum's own credible neutrality. Zones are not built on Ethereum — they are expressions of it.

The claim no competitor can copy: "We didn't build a new thing next to Ethereum. We built the operating surface Ethereum always implied."

---

## Brand architecture

Four-layer structure. The layers have distinct identity — they do not collapse into one brand.

```
ETHEREUM
  authority layer — not owned, not claimed. The source of credible neutrality.

EEZ CONCEPT
  thesis layer — "Ethereum as economic operating system"
  What it is: a framework for Ethereum-native rollup zones, governed by Ethereum itself.

THE ALLIANCE
  legitimacy layer — "we, not it"
  No single team owns EEZ. Shaped by a coalition of aligned builders.

INDIVIDUAL ZONES
  execution layer — nation-state, security-first, low-latency, consortium
  Each zone is a proof of concept, not the product.
```

**Gnosis position:** Founding contributor, not parent brand. Gnosis built it; Gnosis does not own it. The signal is "Built with Gnosis" in the about or partner section. Not on the homepage hero. Not in the masthead. Credibility from the alliance, not from parentage.

**ZisK and Ethereum Foundation:** Co-builders and co-funders. Credited alongside Gnosis as founding contributors, not subordinated to it.

## Visual identity direction

**Minimal / institutional.**

The aesthetic signals standard-setting, not startup launch. Think: Ethereum EIP, IEEE working group, IETF RFC. No marketing polish. No gradient hero. No "ecosystem" illustration style.

**Principles:**
- Structural clarity over visual expressiveness
- Technical diagram aesthetic (the kind you'd find in a research paper or a private GitHub repo)
- Monospace as a design element where appropriate, not just for code
- Color use: restrained. One or two anchors, high contrast. Nothing that competes with the content.
- No icon packs, no stock illustration, no abstract blobs

**What it is not:** It does not inherit Gnosis's playful subversive aesthetic. Gnosis is dry and irreverent. EEZ is precise and deliberate. Same intellectual heritage, different register. The separation is intentional — EEZ should not read as a Gnosis product.

**Visual identity brief:** To be completed by design team. Anchor reference: EIP documentation styling, Ethereum.org research section, not Ethereum.org marketing pages.

## Tone of voice

**Builder-credible, structurally honest, institutionally calm.**

EEZ does not have an opinion about its own importance. It has a position on Ethereum's economic future. Those are different things.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| State the structural problem plainly | Complain about existing L2s by name |
| Acknowledge tradeoffs explicitly | Claim "best of both worlds" |
| Use Ethereum vocabulary precisely | Invent new jargon |
| Let the architecture speak | Summarize the vision in catchphrases |
| Credit the alliance | Foreground Gnosis or any single team |

**Banned phrases (in addition to the guardrails doc):**
- "Ethereum killer" (positioning, not ours)
- "The future of Ethereum" (let others say it)
- "Revolutionary" / "paradigm shift" / "game-changer"
- "Vitalik-backed" or "EF-backed" (neither is an endorsement)
- "Only" (exclusive claims that can be falsified)

**On Vitalik adjacency:** His co-presence at DappCon signals technical alignment — that the model is coherent with Ethereum's roadmap. It is not an endorsement. Frame it passively: "presented alongside Vitalik Buterin at DappCon 2026." Never: "Vitalik supports EEZ."

**On governance claims:** The on-chain governance claim is the load-bearing fact. Every public statement about governance must match the on-chain reality at the time of publication. If governance is early-stage, say so and name the north star. Builders will verify.

## Phase model

**Phase 1 (now): Underground.**
Selective, builder-credible, not broadly public. Alliance is the audience. Messaging focuses on the thesis and the coalition, not the product.

Brand posture: "This is for builders who need it. The constraints are real. The governance is Ethereum's."

**Phase 2 trigger: First zone live on mainnet.**
When there is something deployed to point to, the brand can scale. Not before. Going broad before mainnet creates fragmentation-risk perception — builders default to Arbitrum, Base, or OP Stack.

Brand posture at Phase 2: "Here is what is working. Here is what is unsolved. Here is how you build in it."

**The transition:** The "exclusive underground" aesthetic becomes performative if maintained past Phase 2. Plan the brand evolution before you need it. Institutional calm scales; manufactured exclusivity does not.

## Audience sequencing

1. **Alliance partners** (now, next 60 days) — lighthouses. Public brand only has leverage when partners have shipped.
2. **Ethereum core community** (DappCon + 3 months) — technical peer validation. Credibility is concrete when partners have moved.
3. **Application builders** (post-mainnet) — zone selection only matters when zones have liquidity and users.

---

## DappCon as brand event

The panel (Friederike Ernst + Vitalik Buterin) is a first-impression moment at the highest possible signal level. Brand deliverables required before the panel:

1. Speaker briefing — talking points, anti-patterns, approved examples per pillar
2. Visual assets — teaser + live + recap social graphics, consistent with this identity direction
3. Technical brief — one page, builder-facing, remixed from comms framework and technical spec
4. Alliance partner enablement kit — copy modules per pillar, pre-written threads, quote library

Post-panel: Mirror synthesis essay within 3 days. Partner second-wave amplification in following 2 weeks (each partner writes their own angle, not EEZ retweets).

## What this strategy does not cover

- Logo, color palette, typography — to be completed by design team using the direction above
- Zone-level brand guidelines — requires a separate zone identity framework once zones have names
- Partner enablement kit — separate document, derived from this strategy
- Competitive contrast brief — internal document naming OP Stack, AggLayer, Arbitrum, zkSync Elastic Chain and stating what EEZ is not
