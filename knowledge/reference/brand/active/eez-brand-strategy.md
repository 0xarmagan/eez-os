# EEZ Brand Strategy

**Status:** Draft — internal working document
**Owner:** Armagan Ercan (EEZ Coordinator and comms lead)
**Last updated:** 2026-05-29

---

## Why brand

EEZ has no enforcement mechanism. No company. No foundation. No token incentives. No mandatory adoption path. It cannot make anyone do anything.

In that context, brand is not marketing. **Brand is EEZ's coordination infrastructure.**

Three specific problems it solves:

**1. Alignment without authority.**
EEZ is asking independent teams — block builders, ZK teams, DeFi protocols, infrastructure providers — to align on a standard that is not mandatory, not live, and competes with paths already working for them. They align when association with EEZ signals something valuable to their own audiences: credibility, legitimacy, access to the right room. Brand produces that signal. It makes alignment legible and defection costly.

**2. Credibility before proof.**
EEZ's central claim — that Ethereum can govern its own economic zones — is forward-looking. The on-chain reality does not fully prove it yet. In the gap between "we claim this" and "you can verify this on-chain," brand is what holds the credibility together. Without it, the claim is a whitepaper. With it, the claim is a standard in progress.

**3. Preempting misreading.**
If EEZ does not define itself, others will. Teams threatened by the thesis will frame EEZ as a Gnosis product, another L2, governance theater. The Ethereum community will pattern-match it to things it already knows and is skeptical of. Brand runs ahead of the misreading.

Everything downstream — architecture, visual identity, tone, phases — serves these three functions. If a brand decision does not address at least one of them, it is not a priority.

## The ownable claim

**Ethereum stops being a single chain. It becomes an economic operating system.**

This is the thesis, not a current fact — it is where EEZ is taking Ethereum over an 18–24 month journey. Stated as a direction, not a fait accompli. The claim describes a world worth building toward, before the audience needs to understand the architecture.

**What makes it EEZ's claim specifically.** This is not a general Ethereum vision — it is a precise architectural argument. Ethereum becomes an economic OS through zones that are governed by Ethereum itself, not by foundations or companies. Every competitor proposes infrastructure you adopt. EEZ proposes infrastructure Ethereum governs. That distinction is what makes the economic OS thesis EEZ's to own, not Ethereum Foundation's, not any L2 team's.

The structural anchor: "We didn't build a new thing next to Ethereum. We built the operating surface Ethereum always implied." Zones are not built on Ethereum — they are expressions of it. Governed by the same processes, the same credible neutrality, the same client diversity. No multisigs, no upgrade keys, no admin backdoors.

**Coordination function.** The world-describing claim gives alliance partners a shared position they can hold in their own voice. "We are building in the Ethereum economic OS" is a sentence any partner can say authentically, from their own domain. EEZ is the name for the coalition of teams who, together, are building that world.

## Brand architecture

Four-layer structure. The layers have distinct identity — they do not collapse into one brand. Each layer has a coordination role and specific behavior when events occur.

```
ETHEREUM
  Authority layer — not owned, not claimed.
  The source of credible neutrality. EEZ borrows legitimacy by being genuinely
  governed by Ethereum, not by claiming association with it.
  → When Ethereum researchers or core devs engage with EEZ publicly:
    amplify without over-claiming. "Technical alignment" not "endorsement."

EEZ CONCEPT
  Thesis layer — "Ethereum as economic operating system."
  Coordinates intellectual framing. Gives partners shared vocabulary.
  → When the thesis is stated publicly: Friederike and Jordi lead.
    EEZ account amplifies. Partners adopt the vocabulary in their own words.
  → When the thesis is challenged: engage technically, not defensively.
    Acknowledge what is still being built.

THE ALLIANCE
  Legitimacy layer — "we, not it."
  The alliance composition IS the credibility argument.
  → When a partner joins: both sides announce. EEZ adds them to eez.io/alliance.
    Partner receives the EEZ Alliance mark. Both describe the partnership
    from their own domain — EEZ does not speak for the partner.
  → When a partner goes quiet or misaligns: mark display rights are reviewed.
    Membership is not permanent. EEZ controls the credential.

INDIVIDUAL ZONES
  Execution layer — each zone proves the thesis.
  Zones are proofs of concept, not the product.
  → When a zone launches: zone team owns the launch narrative.
    EEZ publishes the governance spec. Alliance partners amplify from their domain.
    EEZ account credits the zone, does not lead the launch.
  → When a zone is described externally: "an EEZ zone" or "part of EEZ" —
    not a standalone chain. The EEZ mark appears in zone properties.
```

**Gnosis position:** Founding contributor, not parent brand. Gnosis built it; Gnosis does not own it. Credit appears in the about or partner section — not on the homepage hero, not in the masthead.

**Gnosis interface guidance.** The tonal gap between Gnosis (playful subversive) and EEZ (cinematic, structural) is real and needs active management at the points where both brands appear together:

- When Gnosis amplifies EEZ content: Gnosis uses EEZ's vocabulary, not Gnosis's register. The amplification sounds like EEZ, not like Gnosis talking about one of its products.
- When EEZ appears on Gnosis properties (gnosis.io, Gnosis X, Gnosis blog): EEZ is credited as a founding initiative, not a Gnosis product. "Gnosis co-initiated EEZ" — not "Gnosis's EEZ."
- When both brands appear in the same context (event materials, partner decks): EEZ leads. Gnosis is in the contributor credits alongside ZisK and the Ethereum Foundation.
- When someone clicks through from EEZ to Gnosis: the tonal shift is unavoidable. Mitigate by making the Gnosis credit link to a specific Gnosis page about EEZ involvement — not Gnosis's homepage — so the context carries.

**Gnosis operational role.** Armagan Ercan is the EEZ Coordinator and comms lead, operating as EEZ's voice. Ben Carvill is Head of Ecosystem and DAO at Gnosis Ltd — a distinct role within the Gnosis organisation. The @EthEconomicZone account is EEZ's account; EEZ's positioning governs it. This distinction must be maintained in all external communications — if EEZ reads as a Gnosis account, the "we, not it" claim fails.

**ZisK and Ethereum Foundation:** Co-builders and co-funders. Credited alongside Gnosis as founding contributors, not subordinated to it.

**Friederike Ernst as intellectual voice:** For the manifesto phase (DappCon and immediately after), Friederike's public thinking is the closest thing EEZ has to a brand voice. EEZ's own account amplifies and references — it does not lead. This is a deliberate communication architecture: credibility flows from people, not accounts.

### Alliance mark

Partners publicly signal EEZ membership by displaying the EEZ Alliance mark on their own properties — GitHub README, X bio, website, documentation. Being listed on eez.io/alliance is the credential. Displaying the mark is how partners activate it on their own surfaces.

**What this requires:**
- A mark that works at small sizes (GitHub README badge, X bio icon, favicon)
- Single-color variant (black and white, for use on any background)
- Usage rules: who can display it, under what conditions, what it must link to
- EEZ controls grant — partners apply or are invited; EEZ approves display rights

**Mark requirements (for design brief):**
- Legible at 16px × 16px
- Works in single color (no gradient, no multi-tone)
- Identifiably EEZ without the wordmark alongside it
- Derived from the EEZ visual system — not a standalone badge design

**This is the most urgent brand deliverable post-DappCon.** The alliance layer has no external signal until the mark exists. Everything else in the brand system can wait; this cannot.

### Voice ownership

Who speaks for which layer, and when:

| Layer | Primary voice | EEZ account role |
|---|---|---|
| Ethereum (authority) | Ethereum researchers, Vitalik, EF | Does not speak for Ethereum |
| EEZ concept | Friederike Ernst, Jordi Baylina | Amplifies, does not lead |
| The Alliance | Alliance partners, each in their own domain | Coordinates, does not ventriloquize |
| Individual zones | Zone teams | Credits EEZ, zone team owns narrative |

EEZ's own account posts: thesis framing, alliance milestones, technical updates. It does not post partner content on behalf of partners or claim credit for zone-level work.

### Zone identity parameters

Zones are not yet named or launched. When they are, the following parameters apply:

EEZ is open source — any team can build a zone by implementing the standard. No application, no committee approval. A zone that implements the EEZ technical standard is an EEZ zone. The EEZ Alliance is a separate, voluntary coordination layer.

**Fixed (must stay consistent with EEZ):**
- Zone is described as "part of EEZ" or "an EEZ zone" — not as a standalone chain
- Governance claim: "governed by Ethereum" — zone teams cannot contradict this
- The EEZ compatibility mark appears in zone properties to signal standard implementation

**Flexible (zone team owns):**
- Zone name and wordmark
- Zone-specific color within the EEZ color system
- Zone-specific audience and messaging (latency zone talks differently to gaming teams than compliance zone talks to banks)
- Zone-specific documentation and developer relations

### Architecture evolution

The four-layer structure is designed for the current phase: pre-mainnet, alliance-building, thesis-establishing. It will need revision at specific triggers.

**Trigger 1: First zone live on mainnet.**
The Zones layer gains real weight — it is no longer "proofs of concept" but deployed infrastructure. Zone identity parameters become binding, not anticipatory. Zone teams become primary public voices for their zones. EEZ's own account shifts register from thesis-setting to infrastructure-reporting.

**Trigger 2: Alliance reaches critical mass.**
When the alliance is large enough that EEZ is more widely known than individual members, the Alliance layer's role shifts. Instead of alliance composition being the credibility argument, EEZ's own track record becomes the argument. At this point, the "we, not it" framing can relax without losing credibility.

**Trigger 3: Governance is verifiable on-chain.**
When the governance claim — "governed by Ethereum" — is fully verifiable on-chain, the Ethereum authority layer becomes an asset that speaks for itself. EEZ stops needing to explain the governance model and starts citing it. Brand posture shifts from "we claim this" to "you can verify this."

**What does not trigger a revision:** Alliance size alone, media coverage, or price/market conditions. The architecture evolves with the technical and governance reality, not with sentiment.

## Visual identity direction

**One system: the brand extends the video world.**

The EEZ launch video and the brand identity are the same visual system, not separate registers. The video establishes the world; the brand carries it into static assets, documentation, social, and partner materials. They should feel like they come from the same place.

The source world: the "EEZ OCEAN" storyboard. LEGO minifigures navigating a cinematic ocean made of LEGO bricks. High contrast, black and white, epic scale. Individual standardized units (bricks) composing into something vast and dynamic (the ocean). The bricks do not stack — they form a surface that moves. The seams between bricks are visible, structural, always present.

This is the EEZ thesis made material: standardized units governed by the same protocol, composing into an economic environment you navigate. The ocean is not a metaphor chosen for effect. "Economic Zone" is a maritime term. Ethereum is the ocean. Zones are defined areas of it.

---

### Concept

**The seam is Ethereum.** In the LEGO ocean, every brick shares an edge with adjacent bricks. That shared edge — the seam between units — is where the settlement layer is visible. Not the bricks themselves. Not the ocean as a whole. The seam. This is the brand's central visual idea: the connection mechanism is the point.

In static assets, this expresses as: surfaces that resolve into standardized units at close range, with visible seams. The seam color or treatment is the Ethereum signal — the substrate showing through wherever two zones meet.

---

### Separation from Gnosis

The visual separation from Gnosis is achieved through register, not aesthetics. Gnosis is playful and subversive. EEZ is cinematic and structural. Both are technically precise. Both avoid hype. But Gnosis's energy is irreverent; EEZ's is epic. The LEGO ocean world is not a consumer brand or a startup launch — it is an environment. That environmental quality is what makes EEZ feel different from Gnosis without requiring a completely foreign visual language.

---

### Color

**Foundation: high-contrast monochrome from the storyboard.**

The LEGO ocean storyboard is black and white — dramatic, cinematic, no color distraction. The base palette inherits this: near-black backgrounds, high-contrast light elements, no midtones used decoratively.

Zone colors are introduced as the one departure from monochrome. Each zone type gets a single muted, precise color — used sparingly to identify zones, not to decorate. These colors are how you know which zone you are in. They are not brand colors; they are zone identifiers.

| Element | Color direction |
|---|---|
| Background / substrate | Near-black (`#060611` or equivalent) |
| Seam / Ethereum layer | Revealed substrate — the darkness showing through |
| Active seam (composing) | Soft Ethereum purple (`#9080F2` range) — the one light source |
| Nation-state zone | Deep navy |
| Security-first zone | Deep forest green |
| Low-latency zone | Deep amber |
| Consortium zone | Deep burgundy |
| Text (primary) | White or near-white |
| Text (secondary / labels) | Muted zone color |

**Avoid:**
- Gradients on any primary element
- Bright or saturated colors (they break the cinematic register)
- Zone colors used as background fills at large scale — they are accent identifiers only

---

### Typography

**Direction:** Two typefaces maximum. One for text (readable at density), one for structural/display use.

**Text:** IBM Plex Sans or equivalent — engineered typeface, technical lineage, open source, reads cleanly at small sizes and high information density. Not a humanist sans (too warm), not a geometric sans (too corporate).

**Structural/display:** IBM Plex Mono or equivalent monospace — used not just for code but as a deliberate design element: labels, numbering, zone identifiers, metadata. Signals technical authorship without saying it.

**What to avoid:** Editorial serifs (signals media brand), rounded sans-serifs (signals consumer app), custom lettering (signals startup launch).

**Hierarchy:** Few levels. H1, H2, body, label/caption. Information density is acceptable — this audience reads research papers. Do not over-simplify the typographic structure in the name of "clarity."

---

### Logo and mark

**Direction:** The mark is derived from the seam — the point where zones meet and Ethereum shows through. Not a LEGO brick. Not a wave. The structural moment of connection: two units sharing an edge.

This is the brand's thesis compressed to a single form. The mark does not represent EEZ as an entity. It represents the mechanism: the edge where zones compose, where the settlement layer is visible.

**Options to explore with designer:**
- The seam itself — the intersection point where four bricks meet in the LEGO surface, extracted and abstracted. At close range it reads as a cross or plus. At distance it reads as a structural node.
- A fragment — two or three brick-shapes visible from above, with the seam between them prominent. The seam, not the bricks, is in focus.
- Purely typographic — "EEZ" in a precisely spaced monospace or engineered type. No mark, just the wordmark. Credibility through restraint.

**Hard rules (unchanged from prior direction):**
- No gradients in the mark
- No figure/mascot
- Must work at 16px × 16px (GitHub badge, favicon)
- Must work in single color (black-on-white, white-on-black)
- Gnosis mark is not part of the EEZ mark — credited separately

**Priority:** This mark is now the most urgent brand deliverable. The alliance badge cannot exist without it.

---

### Layout and grid

**Direction:** Structured. Grid-based. White space is a design element, not empty space.

- Dense tables and structured information are acceptable and expected — this audience reads technical specifications
- No floating 3D objects, no hero illustrations, no abstract background textures
- Diagrams should look like architecture drawings, not marketing infographics — clear labels, precise lines, no decorative shadows or glows
- Section dividers are typographic or rule-based, not color blocks or gradients

---

### Iconography and illustration

**No icon packs.** Stock icons signal "template website." If icons are needed, they should be drawn specifically for EEZ's conceptual vocabulary: zones, settlement layer, composability paths, governance.

**No abstract illustration.** No isometric city scenes, no floating blockchain cubes, no "decentralized network" node diagrams drawn in a startup illustration style.

**Diagrams are the illustration system.** The composability model, zone architecture, and governance structure are the visual assets. Drawn in a technical register (clean lines, labeled, no unnecessary visual decoration).

---

### Motion

Deferred. Not relevant until there is a website or video asset. When addressed: if motion is used, it should be functional (showing a process, illustrating composability) not decorative. No ambient animations, no scroll-triggered parallax.

---

### Reference points

**Build toward:**
- The EEZ OCEAN storyboard — primary source, the visual world everything derives from
- Cinematic B&W photography: high contrast, structural, large-scale textures
- LEGO brick surface texture: standardized units visible at close range, composing into something larger at distance
- Technical typography: monospace, precise, information-dense — the anchor against pure aesthetics

**Do not build toward:**
- Ethereum.org marketing pages (too polished, not structural enough)
- OP Stack / Arbitrum / Base brand kits (L2 project aesthetics)
- Web3 neon/gradient aesthetic (speculative register, not infrastructure)
- Gnosis.io — same intellectual DNA, different register; visual overlap will confuse positioning

## Tone of voice

**Builder-credible, structurally honest, institutionally calm.**

EEZ does not have an opinion about its own importance. It has a position on Ethereum's economic future. Those are different things.

The coordination logic: a voice that over-claims loses the audience it needs most. Ethereum-native builders and researchers will verify every factual claim. Structural honesty is not a style choice — it is the condition for staying credible with the people whose alignment matters.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| State the structural problem plainly | Complain about existing L2s by name |
| Acknowledge tradeoffs explicitly | Claim "best of both worlds" |
| Use Ethereum vocabulary precisely | Invent new jargon |
| Let the architecture speak | Summarize the vision in catchphrases |
| Credit the alliance | Foreground Gnosis or any single team |

**Banned phrases (in addition to the guardrails doc):**
- "Ethereum killer" (not our positioning)
- "The future of Ethereum" (let others say it)
- "Revolutionary" / "paradigm shift" / "game-changer"
- "Vitalik-backed" or "EF-backed" (neither is an endorsement)
- "Only" (exclusive claims that can be falsified)

**On Vitalik adjacency:** His co-presence at DappCon signals technical alignment — that the model is coherent with Ethereum's roadmap. It is not an endorsement. Frame it passively: "presented alongside Vitalik Buterin at DappCon 2026." Never: "Vitalik supports EEZ."

**On governance claims:** The on-chain governance claim is the load-bearing fact. Every public statement about governance must match the on-chain reality at the time of publication. If governance is early-stage, say so and name the north star. Builders will verify.

## Phase model

Each phase addresses a different coordination problem. The brand posture changes accordingly.

**Phase 1 (now): Prove the coalition.**
The coordination problem: independent teams need a reason to align before there is anything on-chain to point to. The brand work is alliance composition — who is in the room is the argument. Messaging focuses on the thesis and the people, not the product.
Brand posture: "This is for builders who need it. The constraints are real. The governance is Ethereum's."

**Phase 2 (DappCon): Make the thesis legible.**
The coordination problem: the public needs a clear frame before they encounter EEZ through partners. DappCon is the manifesto moment. The brand work is the public articulation of the economic OS thesis — Friederike and Vitalik carry it, EEZ amplifies.
Brand posture: "Ethereum stops being a single chain. Here is what it becomes."

**Phase 3 (post-DappCon, pre-mainnet): Build technical credibility.**
The coordination problem: the thesis has been stated publicly; now it needs to be substantiated. Specs, research, governance documentation, and partner technical work accumulate into a verifiable body of evidence.
Brand posture: "Here is the work. Here is what is resolved. Here is what is still open."

**Phase 3 tone transition trigger.**
The "exclusive underground" posture becomes performative before Phase 3 ends.
Named trigger: when the EEZ Builder cohort exceeds 50 teams, or when RFC participation
generates more external comments than alliance comments in a single RFC period.
Switch to institutional calm posture deliberately at that point — do not wait to be
called out. Announce the shift, drop the "for builders who need it" framing, replace
it with "here is the work." Account cadence shifts from thesis-posting to
research-office-reporting. See `knowledge/reference/brand/developing/eez-phase3-engagement.md`
for the full engagement model.

**Phase 4 trigger: First zone live on mainnet.**
The coordination problem shifts from "can you believe this?" to "should you build here?" Brand scales because there is now something to point to.
Brand posture: "Here is what is working. Here is how you build in it."

**The transition:** The "exclusive underground" posture works in Phase 1 because it is true — the circle is genuinely small and curated. It becomes performative in Phase 3 or 4 if maintained artificially. Plan the evolution before you need it. Institutional calm scales; manufactured exclusivity does not.

## Audience sequencing

The sequence follows the coordination logic, not audience size.

1. **Alliance partners first** (now, next 60 days). They are the coordination proof. Every public claim EEZ makes is only as credible as the partners who have shipped. Brand work here: the alliance itself as signal, partner enablement, making "EEZ-aligned" mean something specific.

2. **Ethereum core community second** (DappCon + 3 months). Technical peer validation closes the credibility gap between claim and on-chain proof. Brand work here: research output, governance documentation, Friederike and Jordi's public intellectual work.

3. **Application builders third** (post-mainnet). Zone selection only matters when zones have liquidity and users. Going broad before this creates fragmentation-risk perception — builders default to Arbitrum, Base, or OP Stack where the outcome is known.

## DappCon as brand event

The panel (Friederike Ernst + Vitalik Buterin) is a Phase 2 activation: the first public articulation of the thesis at scale. Brand design work is deferred to post-DappCon. Pre-DappCon deliverables are comms and content only:

1. Speaker briefing — talking points, anti-patterns, approved examples per pillar
2. Technical brief — one page, builder-facing, remixed from comms framework and technical spec
3. Alliance partner enablement kit — copy modules per pillar, pre-written threads, quote library
4. X teaser threads — pillar-specific, partner-amplified

Post-panel: Mirror synthesis essay within 3 days. Partner second-wave amplification in the following 2 weeks — each partner writes their own angle, not EEZ retweets.
