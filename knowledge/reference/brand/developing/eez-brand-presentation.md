## Slide 3 — Start With What Broke

- Ethereum's rollup bet worked technically. It backfired economically.
- Three symptoms:
  - Sequencing value → private operators, not the protocol
  - Governance → foundation-controlled, upgrade-key-dependent
  - Security → leading L2s carry caveats Ethereum itself would not tolerate
- Ethereum built the platform. Rollups took the economics.

*Note: verify specific statistics before any public use — the structural point holds regardless.*

**Notes:** The $32–40B of TVL across 50+ networks represents real economic activity almost none of which fully accrues to ETH. The rollups that were supposed to extend Ethereum started pulling away from it — sequencing value that should flow back to the protocol instead flows to private operators. The majority of L2 economic activity does not use Ethereum for sequencing. The leading L2s carry security caveats that Ethereum itself would not tolerate. This is a structural problem, not a technical one.

## Slide 4 — The Responses

| Response | What it does | What it misses |
|---|---|---|
| Interop & bridging | Routes around fragmentation | Accepts fragmentation as permanent |
| Foundation-led stacks | Shared technical standard | Governance stays company-controlled |
| Based rollups | Ethereum sequences the blocks | One extraction point closed; others remain |
| **EEZ** | **Zones governed by Ethereum itself** | — |

*Zones are expressions of Ethereum, not products built on top of it. Same EIP process, same client diversity, no upgrade keys.*

**Notes:** Interop and bridging accept fragmentation as a given and try to patch over it. Foundation-led stacks (OP Stack, AggLayer, zkSync Elastic Chain) give developers a shared technical standard but governance remains foundation-controlled — Ethereum-aligned in settlement, not in governance. Based rollups are the strongest competing response: Ethereum validators sequence the blocks, removing private sequencer extraction. But sequencing is one extraction point. Taiko (the leading based rollup) is rated Stage 0 by L2Beat with upgrade keys held by a 7/9 Security Council. Based rollups are Ethereum-sequenced and operator-governed. Sequencing neutrality is real and valuable — it does not restore Ethereum's full economic capture. EEZ addresses all dimensions: governance, sequencing, proving, and upgrade path.

## Slide 5 — The Claim

> *"Ethereum stops being a single chain. It becomes an economic operating system."*

- This is the thesis, not a current fact
- Stated as a direction, not a fait accompli
- 18–24 month journey toward this world

**Notes:** The "economic OS" framing is precise, not metaphorical. An OS has three properties, all of which apply to Ethereum's relationship with zones: (1) it governs the environment applications run in without competing with them; (2) it does not compete with applications; (3) it captures the economics of what runs on it. The third property is the one fragmentation disabled. Windows captures the Windows software economy. iOS captures the App Store economy. Ethereum-as-economic-OS captures the zone economy — fees, MEV, DA payments — flowing back to L1, under governance no single team controls. The honest version of this claim is more powerful than the overreaching version, because this audience will verify.

## Slide 6 — What the OS Analogy Means (and Doesn't)

**What it means:**
- An OS governs the environment without competing with applications
- Provides common ground: governance, security, credible neutrality
- Zones are diverse and specialized; Ethereum is the shared ground
- Composability between zones is native and atomic — not bridged

**What it does NOT mean:**
- One entity controls the OS (no Microsoft, no Apple — governance is decentralised)
- Zones pay platform fees to EEZ (sequencing value flows to Ethereum validators)
- Ethereum governance can arbitrarily change zone parameters

*When the analogy is pushed in these directions, redirect to architectural reality. Do not extend the metaphor.*

**Notes:** If Ethereum is an economic OS, then zones — each optimised differently — don't fragment it, they extend it. Nation-state compliance, security-first, low-latency, consortium coordination: individually useful, together transformative. The composability between them is not bridged — it is native, atomic, settled by Ethereum in the same transaction. When the analogy is pushed toward "one entity controls" or "zones pay fees to EEZ," redirect immediately to the architectural reality rather than extending the metaphor.

## Slide 7 — What Makes It EEZ's To Own

- Anyone can say "Ethereum economic OS" — EF's roadmap implies it, Vitalik gestures at it
- What makes it EEZ's: **the mechanism** — zones governed by Ethereum itself, not by foundations or companies
- Every other rollup ecosystem: infrastructure you adopt, foundation designs, company runs sequencer, multisig holds upgrade keys
- EEZ inverts this: zones are structurally Ethereum — same EIP process, same client diversity, same community review
- The distinction no competitor can copy: you cannot claim Ethereum governs your foundation-led stack. Builders will check.

**Notes:** Every other rollup ecosystem proposes infrastructure you adopt. A foundation designs the stack. A company runs the sequencer. A multisig holds the upgrade keys. EEZ inverts this: the zones are not built on Ethereum — they are expressions of it. The same EIP process. The same client diversity. The same community review. The same credible neutrality. No multisigs. No upgrade keys. No admin backdoors. Ethereum governs the zones because the zones are, structurally, Ethereum. You cannot build a foundation-led rollup stack and claim Ethereum governs it. The governance is either real or it isn't — and builders in this space will check.

## Slide 8 — Why Brand Architecture Exists

- EEZ has no enforcement mechanism
- No company. No foundation. No token. No upgrade keys.
- Cannot mandate adoption. Cannot fund incentives.
- Problem: how does a coalition with no authority produce coherent, credible, compounding brand value?
- Answer: **legitimacy**. In the Ethereum world, legitimacy comes from Ethereum itself.
- The four-layer architecture is how EEZ borrows legitimacy from Ethereum — at four levels, each compounding the others.

**Notes:** EEZ cannot make anyone do anything. This is not a weakness — it is the point. The "we, not it" structure is EEZ's differentiation. But it creates a real problem: without authority, how does a coalition produce coherent brand value? The answer is legitimacy — and in the Ethereum world, legitimacy comes from one place: Ethereum itself. The four-layer architecture is how EEZ borrows that legitimacy at four different levels, each compounding the others.

## Slide 9 — The Four Layers

```
ETHEREUM          — authority layer (not owned, not claimed)
EEZ CONCEPT       — thesis layer ("economic OS")
THE ALLIANCE      — legitimacy layer ("we, not it")
INDIVIDUAL ZONES  — execution layer (the proof)
```

Layers have distinct identity. They do not collapse into one brand.

**Notes:** The architecture is one self-reinforcing system. Ethereum's governance makes the thesis credible. The thesis attracts the alliance. The alliance makes zones viable — builders join knowing the coalition is real. Zones make the thesis true — deployed infrastructure is the only proof that counts. Each layer depends on the others being genuine. There is no shortcut. Brand work cannot substitute for governance work. Messaging cannot substitute for alliance curation. Design cannot substitute for zones that actually launch.

## Slide 10 — Layer 1: Ethereum

- The source of authority EEZ never claims but always borrows
- **Rule: never invoke Ethereum's authority — only demonstrate it**
- How: zones are architecturally subject to Ethereum's governance processes — that is a fact, not a claim
- Vitalik at DappCon: "the thesis is coherent enough with Ethereum's direction that Vitalik is engaging with it publicly" — frame precisely or not at all

Never say: "Vitalik backs EEZ" / "EF endorses EEZ"
Always say: "technical alignment" — not "endorsement"

**Notes:** Ethereum's credible neutrality is the most valuable thing in the ecosystem. It is why Ethereum is the only settlement layer everyone can agree on. EEZ does not claim Ethereum's endorsement — it borrows Ethereum's legitimacy by actually being governed by it. The rule: never invoke Ethereum's authority, only demonstrate it. When Ethereum researchers engage with EEZ publicly, amplify as "technical alignment," never "endorsement." Vitalik co-presenting at DappCon is not "Vitalik backs EEZ." It is: the thesis is coherent enough with Ethereum's direction that Vitalik is engaging with it publicly. Frame precisely, or not at all.

## Slide 11 — Layer 2: EEZ Concept

- The thesis that organises the coalition
- Vocabulary is the earliest form of alignment: "economic OS," "Ethereum-native zone," "credibly neutral rollup" — these terms create a frame. Once a partner uses this frame, they have adopted a position.
- **Voice ownership:** Friederike Ernst and Jordi Baylina lead public articulation. EEZ account amplifies. Partners adopt vocabulary in their own words.
- This is a communication architecture, not a hierarchy. Credibility flows from people who have built real things, not from accounts.
- **Rule: engage the thesis technically, not defensively.** Acknowledge what is still being built. Honest answers build more credibility than overclaiming.

**Notes:** Before there is a zone live on mainnet, the EEZ concept itself has to do the work of coordination. It gives people a shared vocabulary. Vocabulary is the earliest form of alignment — people who use the same words to describe a problem are already more aligned than people who don't. The concept layer also carries the intellectual credibility of the people who articulate it. Friederike Ernst and Jordi Baylina are the voices of the concept. When the thesis is challenged, the response is a technical argument, not a marketing restatement. The honest answer to "has Ethereum actually become an economic OS?" is "not yet — this is where we are on the 18–24 month journey."

## Slide 12 — Layer 3: The Alliance

- The most powerful layer short-term: coalition composition is a signal that bypasses explanation
- Each member made an independent decision — aggregate of independent decisions = distributed due diligence that cannot be manufactured
- **Independence is the condition:** each member must answer "why did you join?" with an answer specific to their situation
- The alliance mark extends this signal into every context partners operate in — GitHub, X bio, documentation
- **Rule: EEZ controls the credential, not the narrative.** When a partner joins: both sides announce, each describes the partnership from their own domain. EEZ does not narrate the alliance on behalf of members.
- Membership is not permanent. A curated alliance that holds its standard is more valuable than a large one that doesn't.

**Notes:** If you are evaluating whether to take EEZ seriously and you see that Aave, Safe, Flashbots, and ZisK are already in the room — the question changes. It is no longer "is EEZ legitimate?" It becomes "what do they know that I don't?" Those judgments, in aggregate, are a form of distributed due diligence that no single entity could manufacture. Independence is the condition: early alliance composition will be founder-adjacent, but each member must answer "why did you join?" with an answer specific to their situation, not "because Gnosis asked us to." A coalition where all members give the same answer is not an independent coalition regardless of size. The moment EEZ starts narrating the alliance on behalf of its members, that independence is compromised.

## Slide 13 — Layer 4: Individual Zones

- The proof that the thesis is real — every zone that ships is evidence the economic OS is a deployed reality
- Zone teams are the witnesses — their testimony cannot be manufactured
- Highest brand risk: one zone with weak governance (multisig holding upgrade keys) falsifies the entire governance claim

| Fixed — must align with EEZ | Flexible — zone team owns |
|---|---|
| Described as "an EEZ zone" | Zone name and wordmark |
| "Governed by Ethereum" | Zone-specific color |
| EEZ mark in zone properties | Zone audience and messaging |
| | Zone developer relations |

**Rule: zone teams own the launch narrative; EEZ owns the governance documentation.**

**Notes:** Zones are where the claim becomes verifiable. Every zone that launches, ships, and works is evidence that the economic OS thesis is not just a DappCon framing — it is a deployed reality. Zone teams are the witnesses. They built here. It worked. Their testimony cannot be manufactured. The zone layer is also where the brand faces its highest risk: if a zone launches with weak governance — a multisig holding upgrade keys, a foundation making parameter decisions — the entire EEZ governance claim is falsified by one counterexample. The fixed zone identity parameters are not branding guidelines. They are the conditions under which the architecture remains coherent.
