# EEZ Brand Guardrails

Hard rules and principles for all EEZ external-facing communications.

---

## Core Brand Values

### 1. **Technical Excellence**
- Every claim is backed by real-time proving and synchronous composability
- Credible over clever
- Concrete examples over abstract promises

### 2. **Ethereum Alignment**
- EEZ strengthens Ethereum, not competes with it
- Ethereum mainnet is the settlement layer and source of truth
- Rebuilding Ethereum coherence, not fragmenting it further

### 3. **Honest Problem-Solving**
- Acknowledge fragmentation as the core issue ("Rollups broke Ethereum")
- Present EEZ as a serious solution with tradeoffs
- No overpromising on timeline or capabilities

### 4. **Inclusivity**
- EEZ is for all L2s, protocols, and builders
- 10 founding partners show coalition, not monopoly
- Open-source infrastructure, credibly neutral governance

---

## Hard Rules

### No Em Dashes
Use commas, colons, semicolons, periods, or restructure sentences.

**❌ Wrong:** "EEZ enables synchronous composability—a feature that changes everything"  
**✅ Right:** "EEZ enables synchronous composability, a feature that changes everything"

### No AI-Tell Phrases
Avoid these banned patterns:
- "As an AI, I..."
- "Let me explain"
- "It's important to note"
- "Delve into"
- "Unpack"
- "Leverage" (unless technical context)
- "Synergy," "paradigm shift," "game-changer"

### No Swagger
Earn credibility through technical insight and plain-spoken expertise.

**❌ Wrong:** "EEZ will revolutionize the entire blockchain industry"  
**✅ Right:** "EEZ restores synchronous composability to Ethereum's fragmented L2 ecosystem"

### No Silent Defaults
Always be explicit about:
- Which audience you're addressing
- What problem you're solving
- Why EEZ is the answer (not one of many options)

---

## Messaging Guardrails

### Do:
- Lead with the problem (fragmentation)
- Explain the solution (real-time ZK, synchronous composability)
- Show the benefit (atomic transactions, unified liquidity)
- Be specific about timelines and tradeoffs

### Don't:
- Claim EEZ is the only solution
- Promise Ethereum will return to 2019 monochain state
- Minimize technical challenges (reorgs, adoption barriers)
- Use hype language or superlatives

## Tone Guidelines

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Technical docs** | Precise, educational | "Real-time proofs enable atomic execution across chains" |
| **Social media** | Clear, enthusiastic, authentic | "One Ethereum. Atomic transactions. Unified liquidity." |
| **Investor comms** | Confident, data-driven | "10 founding partners committed on day one" |
| **Developer guides** | Practical, step-by-step | "To integrate with EEZ, follow these three steps..." |

---

## Audience-Specific Rules

### For Developers
- Focus on composability and contract interactions
- Provide code examples and integration paths
- Be honest about technical complexity

### For Investors
- Highlight network effects and adoption timeline
- Show founding member commitment
- Acknowledge risks alongside opportunities

### For Protocol Teams
- Explain integration benefits (liquidity, composability)
- Address migration path from current L2
- Outline governance and decision-making

### For Retail Users
- Emphasize UX improvements (no bridges, atomic transactions)
- Use relatable examples (Aave + Uniswap in one transaction)
- Simplify technical details

## What NOT to Do

- ❌ Position EEZ as competing with Ethereum (it strengthens Ethereum)
- ❌ Claim EEZ solves all L2 problems (it solves fragmentation)
- ❌ Use "decentralized" as a substitute for "trustless" or "credibly neutral"
- ❌ Promise instant adoption by all L2s
- ❌ Downplay technical challenges like reorg handling
- ❌ Use corporate jargon ("digital transformation," "synergistic solutions")
