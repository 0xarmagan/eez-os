# Messaging & Narrative

What we actually say. The practical reference for anyone writing copy, content, or comms for Gnosis.

Before writing anything, check four things:
1. **"So what?"** — why should a follower care about this?
2. **Does it sound like Satoshi x Deadpool?** — is the point landing last, is the tone dry and direct, would a playful subversive say it this way?
3. **Is every claim accurate?** — every number, stat, product capability, and technical detail must be verifiable and current. Gnosis' credibility is built on technical integrity — one wrong number does more damage than a hundred good posts.
4. **Insight or swagger?** — does this earn credibility through a technically grounded observation, or does it just assert track record? "We shipped this years ago" is empty. Explaining *why* something is hard, or what most people miss about a problem, demonstrates real understanding. OGs don't tell you they're OGs.

## Mother Brand Narrative: Fork It.

Something's broken. We forked it. Or we're about to.

"Fork" is the verb. The object rotates. Fork banking. Fork the attention economy. Fork agentic finance. Fork Monday. Whatever's absurd this week gets named, and we point at the better thing we're building instead.

The ideology (decentralisation, self-custody, disintermediation) is real and it matters. But it's the engine, not the pitch. Nobody buys a car because of the combustion cycle. They buy it because it gets them somewhere.

**Lead with what it means for them. Let the curious find the why.**

| Don't lead with | Lead with |
|-----------------|-----------|
| "We're building decentralised finance" | "Travel without FX fees" |
| "We believe in disintermediation" | "Send money to your family without someone taking a cut" |
| "Self-sovereign custody" | "Your money doesn't freeze on bank holidays" |
| "We believe in ownership" | "1% back on everything. It's the margin your bank was keeping. We just... give it back." |

The ideology is the punchline. Discoverable, not displayed. The tangible benefit opens the door. The conviction is what they find inside.

**When someone asks "how is this possible?"** That's when ownership enters the conversation. There are no shareholders taking a cut. No equity holders to serve. All profit flows back to the people using it. It's not a policy. It's how the thing is built. Ownership isn't the pitch. It's the answer to the question the pitch provokes.

**Bank on Gnosis** remains the key brand line. The double meaning (trust and finance) does the work. "Fork it" is the verb behind everything we do. "Bank on Gnosis" is what we invite people into.

Every product in the portfolio is a different expression of the same verb:

| Product | How it expresses "Fork It" |
|---------|----------------------------|
| **Gnosis App** | Fork banking for yourself. Mint your own money, get the margin back, earn through play. |
| **Gnosis Pay** | Fork the banking backend for others. Infra rebuilt so builders can ship cards without becoming a bank. |
| **Gnosis Chain** | Fork the money layer. Local stablecoins, low-risk DeFi, the rails for a many-money world. |
| **Gnosis Business** | Fork business finance. Crypto-native tools that don't pretend you're a traditional company. |

### The rotating set

"Fork it" is modular. Pick the object that fits the audience and moment. Banks are no longer the fixed villain, which matters as Gnosis partners with financial institutions on the infrastructure side. The pattern is the brand, not a single target.

| Context | Variant |
|---------|---------|
| Consumer, card content | Fork banking. |
| Builders, infra content | Fork the backend. |
| Humane tech / AI content | Fork agentic finance. Fork the attention economy. |
| Cultural / meme content | Fork Monday. Fork the fee structure. Fork it all. |
| Merch / campaign | Fork the system. |

## Product Narratives

### Gnosis App

**The money app where you create your own currency.**

Every human on Circles creates their own money. About 25 cents a day, minted just for being alive. Central banks have been doing this forever. We thought, why should they have all the fun?

This is either the most radical financial experiment in a decade or the most obvious idea nobody tried. Possibly both.

It sounds crazy. It works anyway. And it's the thing that makes the Gnosis App genuinely different from every other fintech, neobank, and crypto wallet on the market. They're all competing to be the best app for *existing* money. We're asking what happens when everyone creates *their own*.

**How Circles works today:** You mint your own Circles, backed by a web of trust. You spend them on your Gnosis card or exchange them for things in the marketplace. Real currency, created by you, usable in the real world.

**Where Circles sits:**
- Fiat = money created by central banks, for central banks
- Crypto = money that rewards speculation
- World = money that rewards... being scanned?
- **Circles = money created by you, spent by you**

Money today is anti-social. We're making it social again.

**The rewards layer:**

Everyone gets roughly 1% back on everything they spend. Not a promotion. Not a limited-time offer. It's the banking margin — the money banks normally keep — returned to you because there are no shareholders demanding we keep it. It's not generosity. It's architecture.

**How the brand shows up in the product:**
- Minting acknowledgment: "You're creating money. No big deal."
- The 1% framed as a receipt from the old system: "Here's what your bank would have kept."
- Notifications with personality, not corporate confirmation messages.

#### Future exploration: Aura and gamified rewards

*The following ideas are not yet in product. They represent potential directions for deepening the Circles narrative and reward mechanics.*

The concept of **aura** — a visible, earned reputation that grows through movement and circulation rather than hoarding — could add a powerful layer to both the product and the narrative.

**How it could work:** Aura accumulates when you keep Circles moving — spending, sharing, circulating. It doesn't reward sitting still. It's earned indirectly, a trace of action rather than a broadcast of status. Not about signalling "goodness" — about being active in the system.

**What it could enable for rewards:** Everything above the structural 1% becomes earned — not by paying for a premium tier, not by increasing monthly spend, but by actually doing things. Streaks. Aura. Circles in motion. Mechanics designed to feel like a game, not a loyalty program.

**Why it matters for the narrative:** Gen Z are more at home in game logic than political logic — feedback loops, streaks, visible effects. A gamified reward layer creates content naturally: people sharing aura, streaks, status. UGC that doesn't need to be manufactured. And aura gives Circles a second hook beyond minting — the first hook is "you create your own money," the second is "and the more you move it, the more you earn."

**Example:** Someone has a podcast studio sitting empty. They give access to someone with high aura who needs to record. They earn Circles. The other person spends Circles. When they need something later, the Circles move again. Everyone gains.

### Gnosis Pay

**Fork banking. B2B card infrastructure.**

Gnosis Pay is B2B infrastructure — not a consumer card product. It enables any wallet to ship a Visa stablecoin card program while maintaining self-custody. The consumer-facing card experience lives in the Gnosis App. Gnosis Pay is the backend that makes it possible.

We rebuilt the backend so any wallet team can ship core banking features without becoming slow, bureaucratic, and custodial.

Card infra that doesn't make you custodial. On/off ramps that don't make you slow. Compliance that doesn't make you a bank.

TL;DR: Banking parity without becoming a bank.

**The problem we solve:** When a wallet builder wants to give their users card spending, they have two bad options. Build it yourself — card network partnerships, banking licenses, compliance infrastructure, on/off ramps. Takes years. Costs millions. And at the end of it you've basically become a bank. Or use traditional card infra — faster, but it requires custodial architecture. Your users hand over their keys. You haven't forked banking. You've replicated it with a different logo.

Gnosis Pay is the third option. Everything a wallet team needs to let their users spend in the real world — without anyone giving up custody. Their users keep their keys. You keep shipping.

**What's in the box:**

| Component | What banks do | What we do | Why it matters |
|-----------|--------------|------------|----------------|
| **Card infra** | Custodial — they hold the funds | Non-custodial — users hold their own | Wallet teams add spending without taking custody |
| **On/off ramps** | Fragmented, each one a separate integration | Aggregated — one integration, multiple ramps | Speed to market without vendor sprawl |
| **Compliance** | Build it or outsource it, either way it takes years | Packaged — regulatory complexity handled | Ship a card product without becoming a compliance department |

**How the brand voice shows up for Pay:**

The audience is builders and wallet teams. They're technical, they're busy, they don't want to be marketed to. Satoshi energy leads here — technical depth, dry delivery, respect for the reader's intelligence. Deadpool shows up in the asides and the honesty about what the alternatives actually look like.

**Important:** Never position Gnosis Pay as a consumer card. It is infrastructure. Consumer card experiences (cashback, "tap to pay", "from eth to eggs") belong to the Gnosis App, which is powered by Gnosis Pay under the hood.

**Competitive positioning:** When consumer card products (Coinbase Card, Crypto.com, etc.) are in the conversation, Pay belongs in the discussion — but the frame is infrastructure, not competition. The move is to invite every wallet to ship their own self-custodial stablecoin card program with Gnosis Pay. Don't compare features to consumer cards. The point: they built one card for their users. We built the infrastructure so everyone can.

> "Building card infra from scratch takes years. Compliance alone could be a full-time company. We already did all of that. You just plug in."

> "Ship a card product without becoming a bank."

> "151 countries. Non-custodial. Visa. One integration. Your users keep their keys."

> "Every wallet wants a card. We built the infra so any of them can ship one — without going custodial."

### Gnosis Chain

**The financial rails for a many-money world.**

99% of stablecoin volume is in USD. 95% of people don't live, spend, or save in USD. Somebody should probably fix that.

Gnosis Chain is where money from everywhere actually works — local stablecoins with real liquidity, low-risk DeFi products, and infrastructure that's been running for seven years with zero downtime.

**What makes the chain the chain:**

| What | Why it matters |
|------|----------------|
| **Local stablecoins** | EURe, BRLA, ZCHF, ZAR and growing. Not just USD with a wrapper — actual local currencies, usable in actual local economies. |
| **Low-risk DeFi** | Yield that doesn't require a PhD in risk management or a tolerance for rugs. Products built for people who want their money to work, not gamble. |
| **Credible neutrality** | 7 years, zero downtime, industry-leading client diversity. Decentralised for real, not as a marketing claim. The chain doesn't belong to us. That's the point. |

**The "Many Money" narrative:**

We're onboarding the world's money. All of it. Not just the American bit.

There's more to money than USD. And as non-USD stablecoins grow — which they will, because 95% of humans need them to — the chain they land on matters. We're making sure they land somewhere that works.

**How the brand voice shows up for Chain:**

The audience is developers, stablecoin projects, and DeFi builders. This is the most Satoshi-heavy surface — technical credibility matters most here. The subversion is quieter: it shows up in the confidence of stating facts without dressing them up.

> "7 years. Zero downtime. That's what client diversity gets you."

> "There's more to money than USD."

> "The reason non-USD stablecoins haven't taken off isn't demand — it's infrastructure. That's the gap we're filling."

### Gnosis Business

**Accounting and treasury, reinvented for stablecoins.**

Your company runs on crypto. Your finance tools were built for companies that don't. You've been making it work — batch payments through Safe, tagging transactions by hand, exporting to Xero and hoping the numbers make sense. Gnosis Business replaces the duct tape.

Trusted by 200+ businesses, processing over $1B in 2025.

**The problem we solve:** Most business finance tools assume a bank account, a fiat payroll, and an accountant who's never heard of a multisig. Web3 teams have been duct-taping workarounds for years. Gnosis Business is the financial stack that actually understands how you work.

**What's in the box:**

| Capability | What it does | What it replaces |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Payments** | Batch crypto payments, maker-checker approvals, invoicing — all in one place | Manual transfers and the "who approved this?" question |
| **Tagging** | Tag and categorise transactions, import chart of accounts from existing tools, add files and notes | Spreadsheets, memory, and optimism |
| **Accounting** | Track crypto cost basis and P&L with FIFO support (WAC coming soon) | The monthly panic of making on-chain activity legible |
| **Reporting** | Export to Xero or QuickBooks, generate balance reports for management | Hoping nobody asks too many questions |

**How the brand voice shows up for Business:**

The audience is CFOs, ops leads, and founders at Web3 companies. They're pragmatic. They don't need to be sold on crypto — they live in it. They need tools that work. Satoshi energy dominates — dry, competent, technically precise. Deadpool shows up in acknowledging the absurdity of the current workarounds.

> "Your company runs on-chain. Your finance tools don't. That's a weird situation."

> "Payments, accounting, reporting. For companies that actually use crypto. Not companies that talk about it."

> "The most bang-for-the-buck crypto app out there." — actual customer quote

### The Voice Behind It

Self-deprecating, values-driven. Punches up, never down. Points out what's ridiculous. Laughs harder the more bleak things look. Life would be easier if we didn't have values.

> "We didn't do it because it was easy. We did it because we thought it was easy."

### Current Landscape Context (March 2026)

**Blockchain:** Stablecoins, TradFi adoption / ETFs, onchain neobanks, institutional adoption, L2s in Ethereum, privacy.

**The world:** AI anxiety (replacement vs augmentation), global political instability, job anxiety, cost of living, money scarcity, social media / big tech / big brother concerns.

**Big tech banking:** Apple Pay, Google Wallet, Amazon financial services, and agentic AI financial assistants all converging on consumer money. Same attention and custody capture pattern as big tech social, applied to finance. The backdrop that makes Human Finance urgent and the reason self-custody stops being a crypto-native concern.

This is the backdrop every piece of content lands against. The playful subversive doesn't ignore it — they name it, then build through it.

## Channel Architecture

Each platform has one job. Don't ask X to be a blog, don't ask LinkedIn to be funny (well, maybe a little).

| Platform | Its job | Primary for |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| **X** | Conversation, commentary, cultural provocation, real-time dialogue | Gnosis (parent), Pay, Chain, Circles |
| **TikTok** | Short-form video — emotional hooks, repeatable formats, irreverent joy at full volume | Gnosis App (primary) |
| **Instagram** | Visual storytelling, community moments, lifestyle — the brand *looking* like itself | Gnosis App (secondary) |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional credibility, B2B — Satoshi energy more than Deadpool energy here, but still direct and dry | Gnosis Business (primary), Pay (secondary) |
| **YouTube** | Long-form: deep explainers, product demos, the "layers for the curious" — underdeveloped, big opportunity | All |
| **Farcaster** | Crypto-native community, governance, ecosystem — can be more technical and insider | Chain, Circles (secondary) |

**The key rule:** X is a conversational surface, not the full content system. Education and deep explanation belong on YouTube, LinkedIn, and owned content. Let X do what it does best — short, sharp, and in the moment.
