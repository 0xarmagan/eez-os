# Audience & Competitive Landscape

Who we're speaking to, what they care about, and what we're up against. This document changes as markets shift — update it when competitive dynamics, geographic realities, or audience insights evolve.

## Audience Segments

### B2C — Gnosis App & Circles

**Primary target:** Gen Z, 18-23 years old.

**Geography:** Initially English-speaking EEA (Ireland, Nordics, Netherlands — not yet UK due to infra). Soon Brazil and Argentina, then multi-lingual. Geographic positioning should be geo-aware — one adoption story won't work everywhere.

| Region | What Matters | Gnosis Angle |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| **LatAm** | Stable value, remittances, dollar access | 80%+ stablecoin market share; real utility for cross-border payments |
| **Europe** | Compliance, "it just works" UX, privacy | EEA card infra, zero FX, ownership without complexity |
| **APAC** | Wallet-native, distribution-dependent | Go-to-wallet partnerships, local stablecoin integrations |

**Psychographic profile:**
- Tech-savvy, emerging into an uncertain future without clear job prospects
- Dissatisfied with "the system" — don't trust government or institutions
- Trust friends and social networks. Discover products via word-of-mouth
- Trending toward micro-communities as they search for identity and belonging
- Strong push for authenticity over polish (y2k nostalgia, not older — but careful not to seem "low tech" for a financial product)
- Never knew "in branch" banking — neobanks are not "new" for them; digital is table stakes
- Increasingly aware the game is stacked against them
- Being farmed at every turn for attention, fees, or another $20 subscription
- More suspicious of perfect aesthetics than Millennials — potential angle for ownership narrative

**The ownership tension:**
Money is inherently transitory — we "hold it" or "have it" but never truly "own" it. It's always slipping away. They don't even know if they *want* to own. Ownership feels heavy; subscriptions feel like convenience.

But owners always get more. And power is fun. Ownership can feel good.

**The rational hook:** The money app it *pays* to use.
- Up to 5% cash back
- Zero FX charges

**The real edge:** Giving back agency and ownership. A system of the many instead of the few. Where power and profit flow *to* them, not *from* them. Our only master is them.

### B2B — Gnosis Pay & Gnosis Chain

**Target:** The new generation of FinTech builders.

- **Gnosis Pay** — plug & play card infra and aggregated on/off ramps
- **Gnosis Chain** — aggregated local stablecoins and DeFi products on a robust chain

We empower anyone who wants to build something more interesting than recreating the status quo on blockchain.

### B2Community — Circles (primary), Gnosis App (secondary)

**Target:** Value-aligned communities of creatives, founders, and builders.

**The hard truths about values-driven marketing to Gen Z:**
- They don't want to be lectured or feel bad. Moral pressure kills participation. They're sceptical of ideologies.
- They don't want to fight "the system" — that feels fringe. They don't want to feel complicit or like it's on them to "fix" things.
- Memes die when they ask people to be better. They spread when they let people feel alive.
- The bigger the claim, the higher the trust barrier — especially in finance.

**The enabling truths:**
- They feel like something is sick. They associate stagnation with sickness. They live with unused skills and unused social capital. They feel blocked.
- Money is visible everywhere but hoarded and extracted. Telling them money is sick and stuck feels emotionally true. "It was made to move" also feels true.
- Focusing on movement and flow takes us out of moralising and into joy. No saints, no sinners. No guilt from getting or resentment from giving.
- They're natural systems thinkers — they "get" mechanics, feedback loops, streaks, and visible effects. More at home in game logic than political logic.
- They want to be seen without performative visibility. Aura is earned indirectly — a trace, not a broadcast. It emerges from action, not self-display. Not about signalling "goodness".

### Who We're NOT Speaking To

- The "winners" of the establishment — the <1% entrenched money that wrote the rules
- People dedicated to rising up and "winning" within a rigged game
- Pure individualists elbowing their way onto life rafts instead of working together to build new ones

## Competitive Landscape

### Neobanks

Neobanks emerged as a rebellion: "Don't get ripped off by banks" (Revolut) or "Help us build a bank you'd be proud to call your own" (Monzo). They capitalised on post-2008 anger, built better products, and talked *to* users instead of *down to* them.

They remain slick, profit-driven machines. Premium subscriptions for best rewards. Always upselling. Hidden conditions on FX rates.

**They haven't changed the game. They just gave it a makeover.**

Key competitors and differentiators:

| Brand | Stand-out |
|-------|-----------|
| **Revolut** | Travel stack (insurance, lounges), 30+ multi-currency accounts, cashback up to 1.5%, integrating Lightning payments and building native stablecoin |
| **Wise** | Transparent multi-currency hub, 40+ currencies at mid-market rates, low conversion fees |
| **Monzo** | Lifestyle/insurance bundles, competitive savings interest, fee-free European spending |
| **N26** | Integrated banking and investing, Wise-powered transfers, insurance/budgeting |
| **bunq** | Multi-IBAN in 22 currencies, 0.5% FX markup, sustainability-focused |

Neobanks are also converging toward blockchain — Revolut with Lightning/stablecoins, Robinhood building L2 with Arbitrum.

### Crypto-Native Platforms

- **Base App** — broad product, yield on USDC up to 4.1%, slick but no clear emotional hook
- **Coinbase** — pushing mainstream, content around grievance/discontent, but optimised for reach vs depth and possibly targeting older demographic with anger/rebellion as lead emotions
- **Krak** (Kraken) — consumer app with staking, global transfers, yield
- **Ready** (formerly Argent) — "Ready to take ultimate ownership of your money?"

### Our Differentiation

In contrast to slick competitors: we are authentic, straightforward, and value-driven. Deliberately focusing on depth of connection, grassroots activation first, and a younger consumer.

**The main difference:** We don't have *users* that we profit from. We have *owners* that we drive profits to. We don't show a good margin to shareholders. We show a good product to them. It's the ownership economy.

Gen Z are more suspicious of perfect aesthetics than older generations. Because our users are owners. Everyone else's users are renters.

## Market Context & Trends

**Stablecoins are credible:** Moving trillions annually. People can use blockchain without believing in "magic internet money". Traditional finance legitimising stablecoins enables mainstream adoption.

**Non-USD stablecoins emerging:** Currently 99% USD but global power shifts will precipitate more local stablecoins. 95% of the world's population saves, spends, and lives in other currencies.

**High grievance and uncertainty:** Life getting harder for the 99%, especially younger demographics, as AI impacts the job market. Cost of living crisis hitting middle and lower socio-economic groups.

**Nation-state declining in relevance:** Activity transcends borders but money remains jurisdictionally based. Blockchain is a global ledger that doesn't belong to anyone.

**Loss of trust in institutions:** ~60% feel the system isn't working and government makes things worse while the rich get richer (Edelman Trust Report). Rise of populism and anti-establishment voices.

**Blockchain abstraction improving:** Easier to hide the blockchain components. Onboarding complexity, brittle recovery, and fragmented flows are being solved.

**Notoriously hard to switch banks:** High trust requirement. General satisfaction with neobank products is relatively high. The emotional message matters more than the rational — Monzo's "help us build a bank you'd be proud to call your own" outperformed product-benefit campaigns by 300%.
