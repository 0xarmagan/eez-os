**Crisis & Issues Management \- SOP DRAFT**

---

**Shared Terminology Guide**

A key part of crisis and issues management is being able to identify and assess what you are facing so that you can ensure proportionality in response.

**Crisis:** A situation that poses an immediate and fundamental threat to the organization’s reputation, operations, or stakeholders.

**Issue:** A situation that requires serious attention but doesn’t meet the threshold for ‘immediate and fundamental threat’. However, any issue can escalate into a crisis if not managed correctly.

**Crisis Response Team (CRT)**

Best practice in crisis management dictates that primary escalation and overall decision-making lies with a single, lean, relevant and expert team. In order to maintain those criteria, certain roles will be part of the CRT at all times, and certain individuals will only be involved if required.

***For Example:***

***Core:***

- Founders  
- Legal Counsel  
- Head of Comms

***Additional, with context***

- Functional Lead(s) / Business Lead(s) (as required)  
- CTO and VP of Tech \- Technical Issues  
- Treasury Manager \- Financial Issues  
- HR Director \- Personnel Issues  
- Community Lead \- Community Grievances

**Critical Success Factors**

- The CRT should first convene within 60 minutes of a crisis being identified.  
- The CRT should reconvene / liaise at least every 2 hours during the first working day, followed by every 4 hours thereafter.  
- The CRT should never contain more than 10 people.   
- The CRT must operate a ‘command and control’ operating model for the duration of the crisis. Only 2-3 CRT members should have decision-making powers – everyone else is there to advise and inform those decisions.  
- All written communications related to the handling of the issue should be retained within closed, private channels.  
- Finally, only the CRT may declare a crisis has ‘ended’ and normal operations may recommence.

**SOP 1\. Crisis & Issues Escalation Procedure**

This section contains recommendations on best practices for crisis and issues escalation. 

* **Detection & Initial Reporting**  
  Any team member who identifies a potential issue reports it immediately through internal channels (e.g., Slack “\#urgent-issues” / Telegram / incident email to the CRT).  
* Reports should include a timestamp, brief description, and source. There is no need to include a definitive threat assessment by the person sharing the escalation.

**Assessment & Escalation**

* Any of the Core Team or Business / Functional Leaders can assess severity:  
  * ***Level 1*****:** Minor issue – monitor within function/operational business.  
  * ***Level 2*****:** Potential impact – alert wider leadership, compliance and comms.  
  * ***Level 3*****:** Confirmed impact – activate Crisis Response Team (CRT).

**Crisis Response Activation**

* CRT convenes within 1 hour of confirmation.  
* Each functional lead provides regular situational reports (SitRep).  
  * SitReps may be templated for speed of production.   
  * Reports should be factual, avoiding inference and speculation unless informed by emerging information, data and/or insight.

**SOP 2\. Rules of Engagement for Crisis & Issues Management**

Once a crisis has been identified and declared at a convening of the CRT, the company’s wider leadership team should be sent a standing SOP on Rules of Engagement to be immediately enacted and maintained until otherwise notified.

***Please note that we are handling a sensitive issue. From this moment onwards, we ask all team leaders to ensure that your teams are aware of the following procedures:***

* No proactive statements or comments are to be made by any employee or any brand channel on the matter.  
* No comments or written statements should be provided to partners or community members.  
* Any client, partner or community questions about the issue/incident should be escalated to the leadership team.  
* Any media questions should be immediately escalated to the leadership and Comms teams.  
* Every request on the matter should be vetted with discretion \- the highest priority should be given to the consistency of message, followed by the proportionality and speed of the response.  
* To founders / execs, please note that everything you publish (whether on X, or informal replies), can be treated as an official statement from the company.

**SOP 3\. Extraordinary Actions for Communications & Marketing** 

In the event of any crisis or serious issue, please be aware that the following SOPs will take immediate effect for all external communications and marketing activities. This is a critical step towards protecting the reputation of the business during a crisis. It also ensures that marketing and communications campaigns do not unwittingly get caught up or tied to the crisis unfolding.

***The following SOP has now been enacted with immediate effect. Please follow your designated reporting lines for SitRep updates or critical escalations:*** 

* Immediate pause of all ongoing marketing and community campaigns and activations for reassessment.  
* Quickly support in the identification and listing of key stakeholders and audiences related to the issue / incident.  
* Implement a robust media monitoring and social listening program to identify, flag, and help to mitigate potential reputational risks early.  
* Identify the potential impact, or not, the backlash / response / criticism may have on reputation and business operations. This will help the CRT to ensure appropriate levels of response at all times.  
* Once response terms are clear and approved by CRT and Legal, respond only using a prepared holding statement.  
* Ensure that any public statement is aligned to brand ethos and tone, governing laws, and any regulations related to the issue.  
  * **Note:** Gnosis operates with the highest standards of transparency. Unless there is legal advice or strong reputational risk to the contrary, Gnosis will always respond to a crisis with this to the fore of our approach.  
* Those responsible for dispersed teams / stakeholders or multiple platforms should fully align with legal and PR teams to ensure that all messaging remains compliant and consistent at every touchpoint.  
* Engage with key stakeholders in a ‘cascade of prioritisation’ to offer transparent updates, where necessary and appropriate, as the situation develops.  
* Conduct a post-crisis review to analyze response effectiveness and refine protocols.

**SOP 4\. Crisis and Issues Communications Strategy**

The Head of Marketing and Comms plays a foundational role in the handling of crises and issues. As standard, and in conjunction with relevant PR agencies, this should include the preparation and alignment of the following tools and resources for the CRT:

* **Single Source of Truth:** A shared (confidential / password protected) document that serves as the single source of all information and facts related to the incident / issue.  
* **Holding Statement:** A brief statement to acknowledge what has happened and assure key stakeholders that it is being handled/addressed.  
* **Reputational SitRep:** A brief, memo-style assessment of the issue’s impact internally and externally, as well as identification of potential escalating scenarios and risks.    
* **Stakeholder Map:** A quick list of the primary and second stakeholders related to the issue / incident, including acknowledgement of any partners, regulators, investors, community members who will require exceptional disclosures.  
* **Spokesperson Designation:** Define and agree a spokesperson for internal and external communications. This will likely also include the decision to have early statements as unattributed to any individual.  
* **Reactive Statement:** A statement that is prepared based on a likely media inbound, that would only be issued if requested and deemed necessary.  
* **FAQ:** Development of a simple FAQ document to preempt and prepare for the most likely questions related to the incident (that can reasonably be answered publicly).  
* **Hostile Q\&A:** Development of potential challenging, dismissive and unfair questions that could arise as a result of the issue to help spokespeople prepare for potential engagements.  
* **Official Communications:** The Marketing & Comms lead should be responsible for all potential content and communications. This includes, but is not limited to the following \- for both the company, brands, and leadership:  
  * Social media statements   
  * Forum posts / Website updates / Blog posts  
  * Media statements   
  * Partner, employee, customer communications

**Critical Success Factors for Communications** 

* The Head of Marketing and Comms and any advising PR agency must be working with all the information available to the CRT.  
* The marketing team should not prepare content that is too far at odds with the brand tone, spokespeople style, or company values – unless otherwise directed by legal.  
* Operational / Technical leads must be open to the input, challenges, observations of the communications experts.  
* How a company communications through a crisis can impact the duration of the crisis \- positively or negatively.
