# Vitalik Buterin and Friederike Ernst on the Ethereum Economic Zone (Dappcon panel)

Source: **Dappcon Berlin, 16 June 2026**, panel: Vitalik Buterin in conversation with Friederike Ernst, closing the EEZ block (15:57). Primary source; this is strategic and positioning material, Vitalik's personal view, not approved EEZ comms. Treat quotes as Vitalik's framing.

## What EEZ is, in Vitalik's words
An effort to rethink what L2s are: keep more of their benefits and fewer of the drawbacks of every approach tried so far, and make something much more deeply integrated into Ethereum, visible to users and developers, rather than a separate EVM chain connected only at the branding level. The benefits of being an L2 have always been tight integration with the Ethereum market and user base, and lighter infrastructure load (a based rollup needs no nodes of its own). EEZ adds synchronous composability between L2s, better bridging UX, and zk proofs securely enforcing it. "A win-win for everyone": more integration, more power, lower cost.

## Categories of chains that genuinely extend Ethereum (not just more TPS)
1. **Privacy.** IntMax, Aztec; Vitalik also counts Tornado, Railgun, and privacy pools as "L2s of privacy" rather than scalability. Expects privacy L2s to move into private programmability as L1 privacy improves.
2. **Very high TPS for specialised use cases.** Lighter; mass-scale prediction markets and other DeFi primitives (he argues most DeFi primitives can be framed as special cases of prediction markets). Ideal on an EEZ rollup.
3. **Integrated oracle.** Underrated. Oracle security is "one of the skeletons in the closet of Ethereum": reinforced-steel doors are pointless if the windows break with one hammer. He argues improving oracles may be higher priority than the push to stage two (stage two comes "for free" with native rollups and ZKVMs). An oracle integrated into an L2 wants the largest, most diverse security base possible.
4. **Enterprise / ultra-specialised.** Start from a centralised, trust-dependent system serving millions (supply chain, finance, medicine, voting) and add proofs to give users better guarantees. Cites Oren's Interfold work.

## Base layer trade-off
Ethereum prioritises censorship resistance above speed, cost, and recourse. Vitalik's caution: if the base layer provides too little, capture happens at higher layers, interoperability gets captured by centralised providers, and apps build lock-in identities. So things like interoperability and censorship resistance need real pressure at the user layer, or you get walled-garden monopolies that wrap the base layer and "virtue signal" being on Ethereum without bringing freedom. He views EEZ as helpful here, because it creates standards so L2s built inside it interoperate by default, not just in theory.

## Nation states and philosophy
Use-case dependent. Do not over-attach philosophy to L1 vs L2 (like attaching meaning to living in a house vs an apartment). Sometimes EEZ is the right solution, sometimes dumping an NFT or an ERC-20 on mainnet is.

## 2035 success metric (Vitalik's pick)
Two or more high-TPS DeFi chains (lighter-style, prediction markets, or privacy) with easy deposit, easy withdraw, and cross composability working between them, plus non-financial L2s, for example DAOs using one chain for governance and another for managing assets.

## For the corpus
- Strong, on-record Vitalik framing of EEZ as deep Ethereum integration and a default-interoperability standard. Useful for positioning, not technical claims.
- The "oracles over stage two" point and the four chain categories are quotable strategic context. Still subject to the usual review before external use.
