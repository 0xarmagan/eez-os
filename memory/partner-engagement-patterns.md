# Partner Engagement Patterns

Confirmed heuristics from the alliance-outreach skill. Apply these without re-deriving them each session.

## Two-asks pattern (launch amplification)

For partner launch or milestone amplification, send two distinct asks:
1. A brand QT (the public-facing share)
2. A personal note from a founder (the relationship signal)

These are separate asks, sent separately, not bundled into one message. Bundling dilutes both.

## Position-first, endorsement-second

When drafting content about a partner, find EEZ's position on the underlying topic first. State that position. Then the endorsement follows from the position.

Do not open with "we're excited to work with X" and then search for a rationale. The position is the rationale.

## Check-in brevity rule

Check-ins are 3-5 sentences maximum. Structure: reference the last interaction or specific commitment from the partner record, then ask one concrete question or give one concrete nudge.

No long recap. No "just wanted to touch base." No lists. One ask.

## Pre-draft research (mandatory for co-marketing and content modes)

Before writing co-marketing copy or content about a partner: read their actual website and any comms guidelines, not a summary. Identify their language and framing. Then set it aside and find EEZ's position in EEZ's words.

This step is what prevents generic campaign-speak. Skipping it produces copy that sounds like it came from the partner's brief.

## Tone calibration for recovery mode

When re-engaging a drifting partner, calibrate tone to drift severity:
- Light drift (just gone quiet): low pressure, lead with value, no guilt
- Serious drift (missed commitments): direct but not accusatory, acknowledge the gap, propose a concrete next step

In both cases: lead with what EEZ offers, not with what the partner has failed to do.

## Channel check

Always check `data/partners.yaml` for the partner's preferred channel and contact before drafting. Sending a formal email to someone who communicates via Telegram signals you do not know them.

## Partner language boundary

Never borrow a partner's campaign language or taglines. If their line is "build together, win together" -- that is theirs. Find EEZ's equivalent claim in EEZ's vocabulary. The vocabulary to draw from is in `eez-voice-patterns.md`.
